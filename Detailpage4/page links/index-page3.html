According to “Study in NL”, an initiative of the Netherlands Organization for the International Cooperation in Higher Education, an International student spends 800 - 1100 € per month on the cost of living in Netherlands.

Fortunately, International students are able to apply for Student Finances. It consists of three components: a loan, a supplementary grant and a student travel product. The only downside is that it only applys for EU/EEA or Switzerland students. The only requirements for applying are having a dutch bank account and be registered with a city hall to get a BSN and DigiD.

The costs of tuition fees depends on the institution and students nationality. For non EU/EEA students the tuition fees are between 6000 - 15000 € per year for a Bachelors degree and 8000 - 20000 € per year for Masters degree. For EU/EEA students the statutory tuition fees are 2314 €. Students starting on a Bachelors degree programme for the first time pay half of the statutory tuition fees during their 1st year of enrollment.
